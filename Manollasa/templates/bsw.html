About course

BSW course or Bachelor of Social Work is a professional degree majoring in the field of social work offered at the undergraduate (UG) level. Typically, BSW degree course has a duration of three years that is further divided into six semesters. In some cases, candidates need to mandatorily complete the course within a maximum duration of six years. Candidates can undertake BSW as a full-time course or even pursue it as a Correspondence/ Distance Education course. BSW course comprises of three major parts including foundation course, elective course and fieldwork. Colleges in India offer a BSW degree course in both English and Hindi medium. Some common topics that candidates need to study in a BSW course include Introduction to Social Work, Social Problems in India, Social Work Research & Statistics, Social Work Administration and the likes. Eligibility

  • The aspirants must pass (10+2) Secondary and Higher Secondary from a recognized board in humanities and science background.
  • Candidates belonging to the SC/ST require at least 40% marks at the qualifying examinations.
